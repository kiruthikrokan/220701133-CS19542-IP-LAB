BHUTAN

Bhutan, officially known as the Kingdom of Bhutan, is a landlocked country in South Asia located in the Eastern Himalayas. It is known for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique approach to measuring national prosperity through Gross National Happiness (GNH) instead of Gross Domestic Product (GDP). Here are some key points about Bhutan:

Geography and Climate

  • Location: Bhutan is bordered by China to the north and India to the south, east, and west.
  • Topography: The country features a varied terrain, from subtropical plains in the south to the Himalayan mountains in the north, with elevations ranging from 200 meters (656 feet) to over 7,000 meters (22,966 feet).
  • Climate: Bhutan experiences diverse climatic conditions, from subtropical in the south to temperate in the highlands and polar-type climate in the north. The monsoon season brings heavy rainfall from June to September.

History and Culture

  • History: Bhutan's history is deeply influenced by Buddhism, which was introduced in the 7th century. The country has maintained its independence throughout its history, unlike many of its neighbors.
  • Government: Bhutan transitioned from an absolute monarchy to a constitutional monarchy in 2008. The King of Bhutan (known as the Druk Gyalpo) is the head of state, while the Prime Minister is the head of government. The country has a parliamentary system with a bicameral legislature.

Economy

  • Agriculture: Agriculture is a significant part of Bhutan's economy, with rice, maize, and barley being the main crops. Livestock rearing and horticulture are also important.
  • Hydropower: Bhutan has significant hydropower resources, which are a major export to neighboring India and a crucial part of the country's economy.

Biodiversity

  • Flora and Fauna: Bhutan boasts a rich biodiversity with various species of plants, animals, and birds. The country has numerous protected areas and national parks.
  • Conservation Efforts: Bhutan is committed to maintaining at least 60% of its land under forest cover and has made significant efforts in conservation and environmental sustainability.
