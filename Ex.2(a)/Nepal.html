NEPAL

Nepal is a landlocked country located in South Asia, bordered by China to the north and India to the south, east, and west. Here are some key points about Nepal:

Geography and Climate

  • Capital:Kathmandu
  • Major Cities: Pokhara, Lalitpur, Biratnagar, Bharatpur
  • Terrain: Nepal is known for its diverse terrain, ranging from the lowland Terai plains in the south to the mountainous regions, including the Himalayas, in the north. Mount Everest, the highest peak in the world, is located in Nepal.

History and Culture

  • Religion:The majority of the population practices Hinduism, followed by Buddhism. There are also smaller communities of Muslims, Christians, and Kirat.
  • Festivals: Dashain, Tihar, Holi, Buddha Jayanti, and Teej are some of the major festivals celebrated in Nepal.
  • Culture:Nepal has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong influence from both Hindu and Buddhist traditions. The country is known for its traditional music, dance, art, and architecture.

Economy

  • Economy:Nepal is primarily an agrarian society, with agriculture being the mainstay of the economy. Tourism is also a significant contributor, especially due to trekking and mountaineering.
  • Currency: Nepalese Rupee (NPR)
  • Industries: Apart from agriculture, other important industries include textiles, carpet manufacturing, and small-scale manufacturing.

Torism

  • Major Attractions: Mount Everest, Lumbini (the birthplace of Buddha), Pashupatinath Temple, Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple), Boudhanath Stupa, Chitwan National Park, and the Annapurna Circuit.
  • Adventure Activities: Trekking, mountaineering, white-water rafting, and paragliding are popular activities for tourists.
