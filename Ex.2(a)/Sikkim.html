SIKKIM

Sikkim, a northeastern state of India, is known for its stunning natural beauty, diverse culture, and unique geographic features. Here are some key points about Sikkim:

Geography and Climate

  • Location: Sikkim is bordered by Tibet to the north and northeast, Bhutan to the east, Nepal to the west, and West Bengal to the south.
  • Topography: The state is characterized by mountainous terrain, with elevations ranging from 280 meters (920 feet) to 8,586 meters (28,169 feet) at Kangchenjunga, the third highest peak in the world.
  • Climate: Sikkim has a diverse climate ranging from subtropical to high alpine. It experiences heavy rainfall during the monsoon season, mild summers, and cold winters, especially in higher altitudes.

History and Culture

  • History*: Sikkim was an independent kingdom until 1975 when it became the 22nd state of India. Its history includes periods of rule by various dynasties and influences from Tibet and Bhutan.
  • Population*: Sikkim is home to a mix of ethnic groups including Lepchas, Bhutias, and Nepalis, each contributing to the state's rich cultural tapestry.
  • Languages*: The official languages are English, Nepali, Sikkimese (Bhutia), and Lepcha. Hindi is also widely spoken.

Economy

  • Agriculture: Sikkim is known for its organic farming. It became the first fully organic state in India in 2016. Major crops include cardamom, ginger, oranges, and apples.
  • Tourism: Tourism is a significant part of the economy, with attractions like the Kanchenjunga National Park, Tsomgo Lake, Nathula Pass, and numerous monasteries drawing visitors.
  • Hydroelectric Power: The state has harnessed its abundant water resources to develop hydroelectric power, contributing significantly to its economy.

Biodiversity

  • Flora and Fauna: Sikkim is part of the Eastern Himalaya biodiversity hotspot, with rich and varied flora and fauna. It is home to the red panda, snow leopard, and numerous species of birds and orchids.
  • Conservation: Sikkim has several protected areas and national parks, including the Khangchendzonga National Park, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
